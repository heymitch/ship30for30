# Day 16: Single Sentence Openers

> **Trigger:** "Build Day 16" or "Single Sentence Openers"

## What You'll Build

Three single-sentence opening lines for an essay, each using a different style (from 6 proven patterns) that creates curiosity and hooks readers immediately.

---

## Build Flow

1. **Read config** — Load `s30-config.md` for student's niche, topics, and Notion DB ID
2. **Read template** — Load `references/D16-content.md` for the 6 opener styles and decision tree
3. **Customize** — Help student generate 3 openers for their essay using the styles that fit their topic
4. **Deliver to Notion** (if connected):
   - Find Day 16 row in student's Notion database
   - Fill the **Template** column with their three best openers
   - Set **Status** to "In Progress"
5. **Fallback** (no Notion): Save to `workspace/day-16-openers.md`
6. **Confirm** — "Your three openers are ready. Pick your strongest one and we'll move to the essay."

---

## Review Criteria

When student shares their three openers, check:
- **Single sentence rule** — Each opener is one complete sentence (not a sentence fragment or compound with semicolon)
- **Specificity over cleverness** — Each opener mentions actual details, numbers, or observations (not generic platitudes)
- **Curiosity creation** — Each opener makes you want to know what comes next (would you keep reading?)

Word count: 1 sentence per opener (varies by style)

Give 1 specific note: Which opener creates the most compelling "I need to read more" feeling?

---

## On Completion

Update Notion Status to "Openers Written" (or note in config). Ask which opener they're choosing and why. Move to Day 17 on subheads.
