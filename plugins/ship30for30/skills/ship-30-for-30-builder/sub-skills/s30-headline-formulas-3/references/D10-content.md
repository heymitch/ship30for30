## Core Concept

### The Three-Part Framework: Topic + Audience + Outcome

**Core principle:** Headlines move through three layers of increasing power:

1. **Topic alone** — "Here's what this piece is about..."
2. **Topic + Audience** — "Here's what this piece is about, and who it's for specifically..."
3. **Topic + Audience + Outcome** — "Here's what this piece is about, who it's for specifically, and what you can expect to get in exchange for reading and giving me your attention."

### The WHY Element (Outcome Specification)

The WHY in your headline is your opportunity to "sell" the reader on why they should read your writing over every other piece of content competing for their attention on the Internet.

**Three guiding questions for the WHY:**
- IF the reader reads this, what do they get in return?
- How is their life different in some meaningful way?
- What will they learn as a result?

### The Attention-to-Outcome Trade

You aren't just asking the reader to "read." You're asking them to give you 30 seconds or 2 minutes of their LIFE (which they will never, ever get back).

Your headline needs to make the trade explicit:
- **Reader gives:** attention, time, energy, mental focus
- **Writer gives:** an outcome/benefit clearly named

---

## Framework: Headline Writing Checklist

Use this simple checklist when crafting headlines:

1. **Be CLEAR, not Clever**
2. **Specify the WHO**
3. **Specify the WHAT**
4. **Specify the WHY**
5. **Twist The Knife**

If you follow this checklist, you will write headlines your readers can't help but want to click on.

Not because you "tricked" them, but because you successfully spoke DIRECTLY to their wants, needs, hopes, dreams, and desires.

## Examples with Outcome Addition

### Example Set 1: Before/After with Full Explanation

**Example 1: Real Estate & Passive Income**

Before (Topic only):
- "Real Estate Buying Tips For 18-Year Olds"

After (Topic + Audience + Outcome):
- **"Real Estate Buying Tips For 18-Year Olds To Start Generating $1,000 Per Month In Passive Rental Income Before You Graduate College"**

Reader interpretation: "Oh, if I read this, the writer is going to give me real estate buying tips for teenagers, specifically to help generate $1,000 per month in passive rental income before graduating college."

## 20 Headline Rewrite Examples (Before/After)

### 1. Betting Big on Retail, Live TV, and (maybe) the Metaverse

**Problem:** Betting Big... but why? What's the outcome? What happens IF you bet big on retail, Live TV, and the Metaverse?

**Original:** Betting Big on Retail, Live TV, and (maybe) the Metaverse

**Rewrite:** If You Believe In The Metaverse, Add These 7 Stocks To Your Portfolio ASAP

---

### 2. Things Men Secretly Do

**Problem:** OK, that's great men "secretly do" these things. Who cares? What HAPPENS when men secretly do these things?

**Original:** Things Men Secretly Do

**Rewrite:** Things Men Secretly Do That Cost Them Their Marriage

---

### 3. Why I Switched to Writing on Substack

**Problem:** Who is "I?" Are you Kim Kardashian? Does the entire world know who you are? Probably not. You need to tell the reader what makes you a credible source of information on this topic.

**Original:** Why I Switched to Writing on Substack

**Rewrite:** I Have Been Writing On Medium For 8 Years. Here's Why I Made The Switch To Substack

---

### 4. 4 Things You Should Feel Zero Shame For

**Problem:** "Things" is broad. Too easy for someone to ignore. There is an opportunity here to lean into a specific "type of thing" people feel shame for, and then tell them why they shouldn't feel shame for doing it.

**Original:** 4 Things You Should Feel Zero Shame For

**Rewrite:** 4 Nighttime Habits You Should Feel Zero Shame For (Like Brushing Your Teeth In The Shower)

---

### 5. Screw Marketing. Choose Art. It's the Safer Longterm Play.

**Problem:** When you read this headline, you can sort of hear the default response in your head being "OK." There's no curiosity gap, and nothing urging you to "find out more."

**Original:** Screw Marketing. Choose Art. It's the Safer Longterm Play.

**Rewrite:** Why Trading Your Artistic Passion For A Career In Marketing Will Give You Depression

---

### 6. Ray Dalio's Business Advice Is Invaluable — Here's How to Make the Most of It

**Problem:** Which advice? All of his advice? Are there certain specific things he has said that are more valuable than all the other things he has said?

**Original:** Ray Dalio's Business Advice Is Invaluable — Here's How to Make the Most of It

**Rewrite:** Ray Dalio Has A Net Worth Of $20 Billion. But These 7 Pieces Of Business Advice From His Book Are Priceless

---

### 7. The Wisdom of Thinking for Yourself In An Age of Experts

**Problem:** Lots of fancy words but do you know what this is going to be about? Do you know what you're going to walk away with after you read this? Nope. We need to double-down on something and get SPECIFIC.

**Original:** The Wisdom of Thinking for Yourself In An Age of Experts

**Rewrite:** In A World Of Gurus And Overnight Experts, Here's An Independent Thinker's Guide To Stress-Test Your Own Beliefs And Think For Yourself

---

### 8. The Hidden Advantages of Working for Yourself by Making Money Online

**Problem:** As a rule of thumb, if your headline is hinting at it being a "list," just make it a list. It's easier for readers to wrap their heads around.

**Original:** The Hidden Advantages of Working for Yourself by Making Money Online

**Rewrite:** 8 Hidden Advantages Of Working For Yourself, From Home, Making Money Online

---

### 9. Don't bother raising a Series A if you don't crush these 3 metrics

**Problem:** This is a pretty good headline, but it can be taken to the next level by framing this less as a statement and more as a question (and hinting at how the reader can answer that question by reading).

**Original:** Don't bother raising a Series A if you don't crush these 3 metrics

**Rewrite:** Think You're Ready To Raise Your Series A? Put These 3 Metrics In Your Pitch Deck And Investors Will Come Running (If They're Up To Par)

---

### 10. Eight Uncomfortable Truths About Life That Will Wake You Up

**Problem:** First of all, if you're going to put a number in the headline, always use the number (8) instead of spelling it out. It's easier for the reader's eye to grab. Second, huge opportunity to "twist the knife" here. What does it mean to "Wake Up?" We need to get specific!

**Original:** Eight Uncomfortable Truths About Life That Will Wake You Up

**Rewrite:** 8 Uncomfortable Truths About Life That Will Put Things In Perspective And Guarantee You Wake Up Tomorrow Excited To Be Alive

---

### 11. Lovely Words That Shaped My Life

**Problem:** "Lovely Words" is vague. Huge opportunity to get more specific here. Bonus points if we can twist the knife at the end.

**Original:** Lovely Words That Shaped My Life

**Rewrite:** 15 Quotes From American Literature That Shaped My Life (And Taught Me How To Be A Man)

---

### 12. You're Never Going to Be Happy Being Anyone Other Than Yourself

**Problem:** It's easy to read this headline and think, "OK," and then move on. We need to frame this in a way where the reader feels motivated to click and keep reading.

**Original:** You're Never Going to Be Happy Being Anyone Other Than Yourself

**Rewrite:** An Imposter Syndrome Sufferer's Guide To Loving Yourself For Exactly Who You Are

---

### 13. Retire Happy With This Much Money

**Problem:** According to who? Who says that's the number?

**Original:** Retire Happy With This Much Money

**Rewrite:** Harvard Business Review Interviewed 27 Millionaires. Here's How Much They Said You Need To Have Saved To Retire Happy (On Average)

---

### 14. Shadow Work

**Problem:** This is a classic "clever, not CLEAR" headline. We need to tell the reader exactly WHAT this is about, WHO this is for, and WHY they should read it.

**Original:** Shadow Work

**Rewrite:** A Beginner's Guide To Shadow Work: Overcoming Fears, Working Through Insecurities, And Starting To Live As Your True Self

---

### 15. If You Fit In, You're Replaceable

**Problem:** "OK, but what can I do about it?" We need to answer this question for the reader.

**Original:** If You Fit In, You're Replaceable

**Rewrite:** If You Fit In, You're Replaceable: 8 Small But Powerful Ways To Stand Out And Go Your Own Way In Life

---

### 16. Why Extreme Wellness Is So Popular

**Problem:** If "Extreme Wellness" is becoming a new term in the zeitgeist, then this headline would benefit from clarifying what it means for the reader.

**Original:** Why Extreme Wellness Is So Popular

**Rewrite:** Extreme Wellness: How The Healthy-Eating Trend Turned Into A Global Obsession With Calorie Counting, Caffeine Addiction, And Creepy Wearable Devices

---

### 17. You Can't Fix Education

**Problem:** If you're going to be a negative Nancy, then go all the way and don't hold back! Huge opportunity to twist the knife here and lean all the way into the opinion.

**Original:** You Can't Fix Education

**Rewrite:** Why The Legacy Education Systems Is Broken, Teachers Are Going To Quit, And The Next Generation Of Children Is Screwed

---

### 18. Anti-Racism Is Becoming Troublingly Racist

**Problem:** This headline teases the conflict, but we can lean into it and really drive the point home. Remember: the reader needs a reason to click and keep reading.

**Original:** Anti-Racism Is Becoming Troublingly Racist

**Rewrite:** The Anti-Racism Movement Is Becoming Troublingly Racist: 9 TikTok Videos That Meant To Do Good But Ended Up Being Toxic

---

### 19. I Lived Through Collapse. America Is Already There.

**Problem:** "I lived through collapse" is a decent credibility statement, but which collapse? Let's get specific! We also need to tell the reader what they're going to get if they read this.

**Original:** I Lived Through Collapse. America Is Already There.

**Rewrite:** I Lost Everything In The Mortgage Crisis In 2008. Here's Why America's Economic Position Today Is Worrisome—And I'm Afraid The Next Crisis Will Be Even Worse

---

### 20. Unreal Estate

**Problem:** Classic "clever, not CLEAR."

**Original:** Unreal Estate

**Rewrite:** How NFTs And Blockchain Technology Are Going To Create A $20 Trillion Digital Real Estate Market (Out Of Thin Air)

## FAQ: Outcomes & Headline Length

### "What if the reader wants more than one outcome? If I list multiple outcomes, won't my headline be too long?"

**Answer:** Remember, word count is a horrible measure of value. Just because a headline is short doesn't make it good, and just because a headline is long doesn't make it bad. What matters is whether or not you are speaking to the reader's wants, needs, questions, etc.

So yes, you can speak to multiple outcomes—just make sure you keep your promise and deliver!

### "If I'm promising some huge outcome, isn't that clickbait?"

**Answer:** No. It's only clickbait if you don't keep your promise.

**Example of clickbait (without delivery):**
- Headline: "How To Make $1,000,000 In 30 Days As A Broke Single Mother With No Job."
- Content: "You have to show up. You have to do it. And you have to work hard."

This is clickbait because the promise in the headline isn't kept in the content.

**Example of legitimate big promise:**
If that's the promise you're going to make in the headline, you better deliver in the content and give the reader exactly what they need to know in order to intelligently strive for that outcome.

Note: You don't have control over whether the reader actually uses any of the information you share with them (you don't have control over the outcome in their life). However, you DO have control over delivering the promised information.

### "Does the outcome always have to be positive?"

**Answer:** No! If your headline is talking about mistakes, for example, then the outcomes would be negative.

**Example:** "Mistakes you made that led to XYZ negative outcomes."

## Summary: Step 3 — Add a WHY

The three-layer framework is complete once you add the outcome/WHY:

1. **Layer 1:** Topic (WHAT)
2. **Layer 2:** Audience (WHO)
3. **Layer 3:** Outcome (WHY)

Each layer adds exponential power to your headline. The clearer and more complete the headline becomes, the more your reader understands exactly what value they'll receive by reading your content.
