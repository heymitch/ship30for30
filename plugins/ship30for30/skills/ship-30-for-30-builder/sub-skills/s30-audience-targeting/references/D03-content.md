# D03: FOR WHO/SO THAT

## Core Concept

FOR WHO/SO THAT is a framework for narrowing your topic and clarifying your writing's purpose. Instead of writing about a broad topic to a general audience with an unclear outcome, you narrow in three stages: (1) Pick your Topic, (2) Choose FOR WHO you're writing (specific audience), (3) Define SO THAT THEY CAN (the outcome you're creating). This three-step process transforms vague writing into targeted, purposeful writing that actually solves reader problems.

---

## Frameworks

### FOR WHO/SO THAT: Three-Step Narrowing Process

**Step 1: Choose Your Topic**

Start with a broad topic or area you want to write about.

Example: "Real Estate"

**Step 2: Choose FOR WHO You're Writing**

Narrow to a specific audience or audience segment. Ask yourself: "Who specifically needs to hear this?"

Example: "Real estate for beginners" OR "Real estate for teenagers" OR "Real estate for people with criminal records" OR "Real estate for billionaires"

**Step 3: Define SO THAT THEY CAN**

Define the outcome. Complete this statement: "So that they can [specific action/result]."

Example:
- "So that they can buy their first home"
- "So that they can start building wealth"
- "So that they can invest without capital"
- "So that they can understand passive income"

### The FOR WHO/SO THAT Equation

**Topic + Specific Audience (FOR WHO) = Desired Outcome (SO THAT)**

When you combine a clear topic with a specific audience and a defined outcome, your writing transforms from general advice into targeted solutions.

## Examples

### Real Estate Case Study: 4 Audience/Outcome Combinations

All examples use the topic "Real Estate" but narrow dramatically:

**Example 1: Real Estate for Beginners**
- Topic: Real Estate
- FOR WHO: People who've never bought or invested in property
- SO THAT: They can purchase their first home

**Example 2: Real Estate for Teenagers**
- Topic: Real Estate
- FOR WHO: People aged 13-19 without income or assets
- SO THAT: They can understand wealth-building starting early

**Example 3: Real Estate for People with Criminal Records**
- Topic: Real Estate
- FOR WHO: Formerly incarcerated individuals
- SO THAT: They can rebuild their financial lives and credit

**Example 4: Real Estate for Billionaires**
- Topic: Real Estate
- FOR WHO: Ultra-high-net-worth individuals
- SO THAT: They can diversify portfolios and manage generational wealth

Each combination targets a different audience with a different outcome, even though they all address real estate.

## Formulas

### The FOR WHO/SO THAT Formula

**Topic + [Specific Audience] = [Desired Outcome]**

Expanded:
- Topic: The subject matter
- FOR WHO: The specific person/audience segment
- SO THAT: The problem you're solving or outcome you're enabling

### The Audience Specificity Formula

Instead of broad audiences, get specific:

**NOT:** "People who want to be healthier"
**YES:** "Busy parents who want to lose weight without sacrificing time with their kids"

**NOT:** "Business owners"
**YES:** "E-commerce sellers doing $100k-$500k in annual revenue"

**NOT:** "Anyone interested in marketing"
**YES:** "B2B SaaS founders who want to generate leads without paid ads"

## Assignment

**Day 3 Assignment:**

Using your topic(s) from Days 1-2, create three different FOR WHO audiences and three different SO THAT outcomes:

1. Write your topic at the top
2. Create three different audience narrowings (three different FOR WHO options)
3. For each audience, define a specific SO THAT outcome

Then write your atomic essay using the Day 3 template: "The Best Way To Get Started Learning About {Topic}"

Your essay should:
- Pick ONE FOR WHO audience from your three options
- Recommend three resources for that specific audience
- Explain why each resource is ideal for that audience's specific outcome
- Make it actionable and ready to implement

## ChatGPT Prompts

### FOR WHO/SO THAT Brainstorming Prompt

**Use this prompt with ChatGPT to generate FOR WHO/SO THAT combinations:**

"I have a topic: [INSERT YOUR TOPIC].

Help me brainstorm different ways to narrow this topic using the FOR WHO/SO THAT framework.

Generate 10 different combinations of:
- FOR WHO: [Specific audience]
- SO THAT: [Specific outcome]

Make the FOR WHO audiences as specific and niche as possible. Make the SO THAT outcomes as concrete and measurable as possible.

For each combination, also suggest what the atomic essay title could be."
