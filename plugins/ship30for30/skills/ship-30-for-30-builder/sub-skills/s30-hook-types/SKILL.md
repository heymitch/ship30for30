# Day 14: 5 Types of Proven Hooks

> **Trigger:** "Build Day 14" or "Hook types"

## What You'll Build

Write 5 different hook types (Frameworks, Stories, Actionable Tips, Lists, Threads of Threads) applied to your topic, each creating curiosity or tension.

---

## Build Flow

1. **Read config** — Load `s30-config.md` for student's niche, topics, and Notion DB ID
2. **Read template** — Load `references/D14-content.md` for Day 14 writing template
3. **Customize** — Fill template with student's topic and create 5 hook variations
4. **Deliver to Notion** (if connected):
   - Find Day 14 row in student's Notion database
   - Fill the **Template** column with customized template
   - Set **Status** to "In Progress"
5. **Fallback** (no Notion): Save to `workspace/day-14-template.md`
6. **Confirm** — "Your Day 14 template is ready in [Notion/local file]. Write your draft, then tell me when you're done for feedback."

---

## Review Criteria

When student shares their draft, check:
- **Uses all 5 hook types** — Covers Frameworks, Stories, Actionable Tips, Lists, and Threads categories (not just 1-2)
- **Each hook specific to their topic** — Hooks are tailored to their niche/audience, not generic examples
- **Creates curiosity or tension** — Each hook hints at a surprising outcome or valuable insight (not flat/obvious)
- Word count (target: ~250, max 300)
- One clear idea, no fluff

Give 2-3 specific, actionable notes. Don't rewrite for them.

---

## On Completion

Update Notion Status to "Shipped" (or note in config). Ask if they want to move to the next day.
