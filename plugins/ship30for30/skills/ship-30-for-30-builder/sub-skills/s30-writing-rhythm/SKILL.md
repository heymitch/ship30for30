# Day 18: 1/3/1 Writing Rhythm

> **Trigger:** "Build Day 18" or "Writing Rhythm"

## What You'll Build

A blog post or essay section reformatted with 1/3/1 rhythm—alternating between single sentences and three-sentence blocks to create visual and psychological pacing.

---

## Build Flow

1. **Read config** — Load `s30-config.md` for student's niche, topics, and Notion DB ID
2. **Read template** — Load `references/D18-content.md` for 1/3/1 rhythm formulas and alternatives
3. **Customize** — Help student identify where rhythm naturally exists in their draft, then format it intentionally
4. **Deliver to Notion** (if connected):
   - Find Day 18 row in student's Notion database
   - Fill the **Template** column with their reformatted piece (before + after)
   - Set **Status** to "In Progress"
5. **Fallback** (no Notion): Save to `workspace/day-18-rhythm.md`
6. **Confirm** — "Your rhythm is flowing. The alternation gives the reader breathing room. Ready to move forward?"

---

## Review Criteria

When student shares their before/after rhythm example, check:
- **Follows 1/3/1 pattern** — Can identify single sentences isolated and three-sentence blocks grouped
- **Varied rhythm feels intentional** — Not forced; emerges naturally from content flow
- **White space enhances reading** — The rhythm makes content more scannable than the original

Word count: Variable (before/after comparison, typically 300-500 words total)

Give 1-2 specific notes on how rhythm changed readability.

---

## On Completion

Update Notion Status to "Rhythm Applied" (or note in config). Move to Day 19 (short-form formatting). Ask if they want to continue.
