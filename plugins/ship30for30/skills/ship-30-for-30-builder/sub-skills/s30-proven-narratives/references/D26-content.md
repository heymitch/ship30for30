## Core Concept

**Definition:** Core Narratives are 3-5 stories, experiences, and qualities that prolific creators consistently repeat about themselves, which eventually become how audiences know and identify them.

**Key Principle:** "You decide what stories and qualities people associate with you."

**Core Strategic Insight:** When you see certain Core Narratives resonate with readers, it's worth doubling-down on them and repeating them as often as possible.

---

## The 5-Category Core Narrative Framework

### Framework Overview

Every prolific creator has 3-5 core narratives that audiences associate with them. These narratives fall into five key categories:

### Category 1: Personal Stories
**Definition:** What things have you done or experienced that made you, you?

**Examples from PDF:**
- Dickie played football at Princeton
- Cole studied fiction writing at Columbia College Chicago

**Function:** Establishes formative experiences that shaped your identity

### Category 2: Personal Achievements
**Definition:** What things have you accomplished? What have you built? These could be in your career or your personal life.

**Examples from PDF:**
- Dickie used to be a hedge fund trader at Blackrock
- Cole used to be a professional World of Warcraft player

**Function:** Demonstrates competence and accomplishment

### Category 3: Background
**Definition:** Where are you from? Where is your family from? Where did you grow up, and how did that shape who you are today?

**Examples from PDF:**
- Gary Vaynerchuk speaks often about the impact being an immigrant has had on his entrepreneurial journey

**Function:** Provides context for your journey and influences

### Category 4: Aspirations
**Definition:** What do you want to achieve in your lifetime? What is a big, audacious goal you have set for yourself?

**Examples from PDF:**
- Gary Vaynerchuk constantly says how he wants to buy the New York Jets
- Alex Hormozi says he wants to become a billionaire

**Function:** Shows direction, ambition, and long-term vision

### Category 5: Fun Facts
**Definition:** What weird things have been part of your journey? What sidequests has life taken you on? What are your hobbies or interests outside of your primary career, your business, or your niche?

**Function:** Humanizes you and makes you memorable through unique details

## Addressing Common Questions & Objections

### Q: "What if I don't know what my Core Narratives are?"

**Answer:** It's a subjective decision. You decide. And if you can't decide, then let the market decide for you. Share lots of different types of stories and see which ones resonate the most—then make those your Core Narratives.

### Q: "But if I repeat my Core Narratives often won't readers get tired of hearing the same things from me?"

**Answer:** Nope. Gary Vaynerchuk has probably created 3,000+ pieces of content telling the story of him and his parents immigrating to the United States from Belarus. And readers (or listeners, or viewers) never get tired of it. It's a Core Narrative.

### Q: "What if I don't want to share anything personal with my audience?"

**Answer:** That's fine. Ryan Holiday doesn't share very much "personal" with his audience either. But a lot of people do know he owns a bookstore in Texas. Even something like that is enough to be a Core Narrative and help readers get to know you a little bit more deeply.

### Q: "Should I repeat my Core Narratives in every single thing I write?"

**Answer:** Not necessarily. Don't force it. But wherever it feels applicable, feel free to re-tell those same stories and tidbits about yourself as often or as little as you'd like.

## Key Principles & Takeaways

1. **Core Narratives are deliberate choices:** "You decide what stories and qualities people associate with you."

2. **Repetition is the key:** Audiences don't tire of well-crafted Core Narratives; they deepen familiarity and recall.

3. **Five categories cover all bases:**
   - Personal Stories (formative experiences)
   - Personal Achievements (competence)
   - Background (origin and influences)
   - Aspirations (future vision)
   - Fun Facts (memorable quirks)

4. **Market testing is valid:** If you don't know your Core Narratives, let audience engagement guide you.

5. **Flexibility over perfection:** You don't need to force Core Narratives into every piece; use them where applicable.

6. **Transparency varies:** You don't need extreme vulnerability to have Core Narratives—even a single memorable detail (like owning a bookstore) works.
