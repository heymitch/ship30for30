## Framework: The Idea File 3-Step Process

The Idea File is the foundation of the entire opportunity flywheel. By completing an Idea File for 1 topic, you realize HOW MUCH OPPORTUNITY you have as a writer—and how you could create one for ANY topic you wanted to write about, and continue to reinvent yourself over and over again anytime you wanted.

### Step 1: Choose 1 Topic

**4 Good Questions to Ask Yourself When Choosing a Topic:**

1. "Is this a topic I know a lot about?"
2. "Is this a topic that I have personally experienced?"
3. "Is this a topic that I can easily generate ideas around?"
4. "Is this a topic I would be excited to talk about with other people?"

**Topic Narrowing Examples:**

- Topic V1: "Journaling"
- Topic V2: "Journaling tips for people who have never started a journal before."
- Topic V3: "Journaling tips for busy college students who have never started a journal before."
- Topic V4: "Journaling tips for busy college students who have never started a journal before, so they can be more grateful and less stressed."

**Finding Your Topic (Using Analytics):**

Check your social profiles for high-level metrics:
- Comments
- Retweets/Shares
- Likes
- Views

Look for patterns between which topics are prompting the most engagement vs which topics are falling flat. Pick a "winning" topic and get started.

**What are "Subtopics?"**

Subtopics are the things you can write about "inside" that 1 umbrella topic you've chosen. They are always action-oriented, beginning with a verb.

**Examples of Subtopics (Verb-Based):**

- Mastering...
- Developing...
- Improving...
- Building...
- Overcoming...
- Achieving...
- Optimizing...

Subtopics are verbs because whatever it is you want to write about (the Topic) has to be aimed at a reader's wants, needs, and desires. ### Step 3: Use ChatGPT To Generate Proven Approaches For Each Of The 7 Subtopics (For 1 Topic)

**Generating Proven Approaches (Per Subtopic)**

If Subtopics are "verbs," then Proven Approaches are "nouns." They are the things readers want/need IN ORDER TO achieve the outcome/verb.

**Quick Examples:**

- Tips (noun) → ...to build (verb).
- Mistakes (noun) → ...to avoid (verb).
- Lessons (noun) → ...to achieve (verb).

Etc.

This little equation is the easiest way to start generating an endless amount of ideas on any given topic.

**Proven Approaches Framed As Questions**

It's even easier if each Proven Approach is framed as a question.

**Example:**

Topic: Premium Ghostwriting

As soon as it's framed as a question, it's much easier to imagine yourself "writing the answer."
