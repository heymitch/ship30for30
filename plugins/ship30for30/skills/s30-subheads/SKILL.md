# Day 17: The Art of Subheads

> **Trigger:** "Build Day 17" or "Art of Subheads"

## What You'll Build

3-5 strategically aligned subheads for an essay that directly answer the headline's promise, follow a consistent pattern, and pass the visual scanning test.

---

## Build Flow

1. **Read config** — Load `s30-config.md` for student's niche, topics, and Notion DB ID
2. **Read template** — Load `references/D17-content.md` for subhead matching formula and pattern options
3. **Customize** — Help student map subheads using one of three patterns: Mistakes, Reasons, or Hard Decisions
4. **Deliver to Notion** (if connected):
   - Find Day 17 row in student's Notion database
   - Fill the **Template** column with their aligned subheads
   - Set **Status** to "In Progress"
5. **Fallback** (no Notion): Save to `workspace/day-17-subheads.md`
6. **Confirm** — "Your subheads are solid. Read headline + subheads alone—do they tell the full story?"

---

## Review Criteria

When student shares their subheads with headline, check:
- **Subheads guide the scanner** — Can someone read only headline + subheads and understand your argument?
- **Each subhead is self-explanatory** — No subhead is vague or requires the body to understand what it means
- **Consistent style** — All subheads follow the same pattern (all "Mistake #X", all "Why" statements, etc.)

Word count: Headline + 3-5 subheads only

Give 1-2 specific notes on alignment or consistency.

---

## On Completion

Update Notion Status to "Subheads Mapped" (or note in config). Move to Day 18 (writing rhythm). Ask if they want to continue.
