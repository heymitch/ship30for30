# Day 01: The Anatomy of an Atomic Essay

> **Trigger:** "Build Day 01" or "Write atomic essay"

## What You'll Build

Your first atomic essay titled "Why I'm Starting To Write Online"—a self-contained 250-300 word piece expressing your core motivation and three topics you'll explore.

---

## Build Flow

1. **Read config** — Load `s30-config.md` for student's niche and Notion DB ID
2. **Read template** — Load `references/D01-content.md` for Day 1 writing template
3. **Customize** — Fill "Why I'm Starting To Write Online" template with student's motivation
4. **Deliver to Notion** (if connected):
   - Find Day 01 row in student's Notion database
   - Fill **Template** column with customized template
   - Set **Status** to "In Progress"
5. **Fallback** (no Notion): Save to `workspace/day-01-essay.md`
6. **Confirm** — "Your Day 01 template is ready. Write your draft explaining your three reasons and three topics. Tell me when you're done for feedback."

---

## Review Criteria

When student shares their draft, check:
- **One core idea**: Is there ONE clear reason they're writing (not scattered)?
- **Self-contained**: Would someone reading this alone understand their full purpose?
- **Personal motivation**: Does it show genuine interest in the topics (not generic "I want to help people")?
- Word count target: 250 words, max 300
- Clear connection between reasons and topics

Give 2-3 specific notes. Don't rewrite for them.

---

## On Completion

Update Notion Status to "Shipped" (or note in config). Ask if they're ready for Day 02 (Topic Selection with 2-Year Test).
