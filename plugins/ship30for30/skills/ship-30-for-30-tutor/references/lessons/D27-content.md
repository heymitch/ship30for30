## Core Concept

Your bio is the first impression visitors get when they land on your profile. This lesson focuses on the 4 most common mistakes people make when crafting their Twitter/X bios that undermine credibility, clarity, and audience connection.

## Frameworks

### The 4 Bio Mistakes Framework

**Mistake #1: Only listing "what you do."**

- Problem: Treating bio as a credentials list
- Example: "CEO @companyname, Board Member @companyname, Investor @companyname"
- Why it fails: Answers WHO you are, not WHY anyone should follow
- Principle: Credibility alone doesn't answer "What sort of content do you create?"

**Mistake #2: Trying to be Clever, not CLEAR**

- Problem: Using wordplay, quotes, or obscure references
- Example: "Netflix, Pancakes, And Stephen King novels. Don't bother me, I'm writing. Who are they? No clue. What do they do? No idea."
- Why it fails: Leaves readers confused about your actual focus and value proposition
- Principle: Clarity beats cleverness in bio space. You have 160 characters for X (Twitter).

**Mistake #3: Saying "just sharing what I'm learning"**

- Problem: Using hedging language that signals uncertainty
- Example: "Just sharing what I'm learning along the way."
- Why it fails: Implicitly tells readers "I don't really know who I am yet. Or what I do. Or what content you can expect from me. So, if you want to follow me, go ahead. But honestly, I can't promise you anything yet."
- Principle: Even if you're a beginner, PLANT YOUR FLAG. Claim where you are. Own it.
- Action: Instead, state clearly: "I write about productivity hacks for total beginners."

**Mistake #4: Not updating their profile picture or banner image**

- Problem: Outdated or low-quality visuals signal lack of intention
- Signals readers make:
  - "This person isn't playing the game of digital writing with intention."
  - "If this person isn't playing the game with intention, why should I follow them?"
  - "Is this person a bot?"
- Principle: Professionalism and intention signal movement. People follow those moving forward.

## Checklists

### 4 Bio Mistakes Evaluation Checklist

- [ ] Am I only listing "what I do" without explaining the TOPIC I write about?
- [ ] Am I trying to be clever instead of CLEAR?
- [ ] Am I saying "just learning" or hedging my authority?
- [ ] Do I have a professional, current profile picture and banner image?

### Profile Picture Requirements

- [ ] Have a photo (not a logo or silhouette)
- [ ] Photo is current (not 11+ years old)
- [ ] Photo is high quality (not blurry)
- [ ] Photo is not a distant silhouette (100 yards away)
- [ ] Phone headshot on white wall is sufficient

## Formulas/Patterns

### Bio Structure to Avoid: Mistake #1

Pattern to recognize and eliminate:
```
Title @company, Position @company, Role @company. [Random accomplishment]. [Random accomplishment].
```

Replace with answer to: "What topic/topics do you write about?"

### Bio Structure to Avoid: Mistake #3

Pattern to recognize and eliminate:
```
"Just sharing what I'm learning..."
OR
"Still figuring it out..."
OR
"Learning to become a [topic]..."
```

Replace with: "I write about [Topic]" or "I talk about [Topic]"

## Examples

### Mistake #1 Example: Only Credentials

**Bad Bio:**
"CEO @companyname, Board Member @companyname, Investor @companyname, @companyname. This is a bio that has over-tilted on credibility, and is basically telling the reader, "Hey, I don't need to tell you who I am OR what you can expect from me. You should just know. See all my credentials?" And this is a giant mistake. Because credibility, itself, doesn't answer the question, "Yea but, what sort of content do you create? Why should I pay attention to you?""

**Core Issue:** Credibility without context leaves readers lost about your actual value.

### Mistake #2 Example: Trying to Be Clever

**Bad Bio:**
"Netflix, Pancakes, And Stephen King novels. Don't bother me, I'm writing. Who are they? No clue. What do they do? No idea. What can you expect from them? Who knows."

**Why it fails:** Reader literally has no idea what this person writes about. Being clever obscures clarity.

### Mistake #3 Example: "Just Learning" Hedging

**Bad Bio:**
"Just sharing what I'm learning along the way."

**Subtext Read by Readers:** "Hey, just a heads up, I don't really know who I am yet. Or what I do. Or what sort of content you can expect from me. So, if you want to follow me, go ahead. But honestly, I can't promise you anything yet."

**Result:** Why would someone follow someone who can't promise value?

### Mistake #4 Example: Outdated Visuals

**Signal Analysis:** Not having a clean profile picture and banner image causes readers to assume:
- This person isn't intentional
- This person isn't moving forward positively
- This person might be a bot

## Questions (FAQ)

**Q: "What if I don't have a huge budget to get professional photos taken & banner images designed?"**

A: You don't need expensive professional photography. Have a friend, co-worker, or family member take a new picture of you on your phone in front of a white wall. Then grab a high-quality image off Google and add it as your banner image. Done!

**Q: "My favorite creator has a clever bio and I really like it—why can't I use that too?"**

A: Did they build their audience BECAUSE of that? Or DESPITE of that? We would argue they would see even more growth and traction if they followed the rules we've outlined here for you.
