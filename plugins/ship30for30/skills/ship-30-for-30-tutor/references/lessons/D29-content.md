## Core Concept

Pinning content to your X (Twitter) and LinkedIn profile is the final step in profile optimization. Your pinned asset is something every single person who comes to your profile is going to see. This is the moment to communicate what you're all about—who you are, what you do, and what potential followers can expect from you. Just like your bio, your pinned asset should say something about you.

## Frameworks

### Framework #1: Pinning Strategy - X (Twitter)

**Core Principle:**
At the top of your X (Twitter) profile, you have the option to "pin" a Tweet (or a Thread).

**Why Pin Content:**
The reason you want to "pin" something to your profile is because, just like your bio, this first Tweet is something every single person who comes to your profile is going to see.

So, you want your "pinned" asset to say something about you.

**Key Rule:**
On X (Twitter) you can only pin 1 thing to your profile, whereas on LinkedIn you can pin multiple.

---

### Framework #2: Things You Can Pin - 6 Options

**Option #1: The Most Viral Piece You've Written**
- Signals social credibility and engagement
- Example: Highest view count, most shares

**Option #2: A Thread on a Topic You Want to Be Associated With**
- Further solidifies your niche/category
- Example: Multi-tweet breakdown of expertise

**Option #3: A Thread That Tells Your Story**
- Links to all your different products/companies
- Gives readers a "crash course" on who you are and what you do

**Option #4: A Recent Business, Product, or Book Launch**
- Brings more visibility to something you're actively promoting
- Example: New course, book, or service launch

**Option #5: A Video, a Podcast Interview, Etc.**
- Gives readers access to you through a different medium other than text
- Increases accessibility and connection points

**Option #6: Etc.**
- Any other content that accomplishes your goals
- You have flexibility; choose what best represents you

**Big Idea:**
The big idea here is to give new potential followers another way to understand who you are and what you're all about.

**Important Note:**
And yes, this pinned asset can (and likely should) change over time.

---

### Framework #3: Pinning on LinkedIn

**Core Principle:**
You can do the same thing on LinkedIn.

**Key Difference:**
Except on X (Twitter) you can only pin 1 thing to your profile, whereas on LinkedIn you can pin multiple.

**Examples of Featured Content on LinkedIn:**
- Start Writing Online (free ultimate guide)
- Premium Ghostwriting Blueprint (free email course for ghostwriters)
- Write With AI (paid newsletter for AI writing)
- Ship 30 for 30 (core product/course)
- Personal newsletters (individual creator offerings)

## Formulas/Patterns

### Formula #1: X (Twitter) Pinning Strategy

**Single Pin Rule:**
```
Profile Pin (1 max) = Your strategic first impression
```

**Options:**
- Most viral (credibility signal)
- Topic thread (category solidification)

**Multiple Pins Allowed:**

**Content Types:**
- External links (websites, opt-ins, products)
- Blog posts
- Newsletters
- Courses/Programs

**Click Hierarchy:**

## Questions (FAQ)

**Q: "What if I want to change the thing I've pinned or am promoting? How often can I do that?"**

A: As often as you'd like. You might not want to change it every single day (it's worth keeping something pinned for at least a few weeks), but it's completely up to you. And remember: the thing you pin to your profile is most likely to get a lot of visibility & link clicks. So, the longer you leave it pinned, the more people who are going to see it!

**Q: "What is the best type of thing to pin?"**

A: There really isn't anything that is "the best." What matters is that the pinned asset gives readers more insight into who you are and what you can do for THEM. How you can help THEM.

## Platform Differences Summary

### X (Twitter)
- 1 pin maximum per profile
- Most visible real estate
- Highest click-through rate
- Best for: Your absolute best/most strategic piece

### LinkedIn
- Multiple pins allowed
- Featured section visible
- Secondary to short-form bio link
- Best for: Portfolio of offerings (free guides, paid products, etc.)
- Can feature websites, opt-ins, products, newsletters, courses, books
